# Pillsbury Doughboy Amigurumi Crochet Pattern

*An unofficial fan-made pattern inspired by the iconic Pillsbury Doughboy character.*
*All terms are USA standard.*

---

## Finished Size

Approximately **6 inches tall** (stuffed, standing)

---

## Materials

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Yarn | Worsted weight (size 4) — white or off-white, approx. 100 yards |
| Accent yarn | Small amount of light blue (for scarf/kerchief) |
| Accent yarn | Small amount of white for chef hat |
| Accent yarn | Small amount of gray or silver for hat band |
| Hook | Size G-6 (4.0 mm) |
| Safety eyes | 9 mm black safety eyes × 2 |
| Stuffing | Polyester fiberfill |
| Yarn needle | For sewing and weaving in ends |
| Stitch markers | Optional but recommended |
| Pins | For positioning pieces before sewing |

---

## Abbreviations (USA Terms)

| Abbreviation | Meaning |
|---|---|
| MC | Magic circle (magic ring) |
| ch | Chain |
| sl st | Slip stitch |
| sc | Single crochet |
| hdc | Half double crochet |
| dc | Double crochet |
| inc | Increase: 2 sc in same stitch |
| dec | Invisible decrease: (insert hook in front loop of next 2 sts, pull through both, yarn over, pull through 2 loops) |
| BLO | Back loop only |
| FLO | Front loop only |
| st(s) | Stitch(es) |
| rnd | Round |
| rep | Repeat |
| () | Work all instructions inside parentheses in the same stitch |

---

## Gauge

5 sc × 5 rows = 1 inch

*Gauge is not critical for amigurumi, but a tighter gauge produces a firmer fabric that holds stuffing better. If your stitches look too loose, try a smaller hook.*

---

## Pattern Notes

- All pieces are worked in continuous rounds (no joining) unless otherwise noted. Use a stitch marker to mark the beginning of each round.
- Stuff as you go — don't wait until the piece is finished to add fiberfill.
- Use the invisible decrease for a smooth finish on curves.
- Safety eyes must be inserted **before** the opening is too small to reach inside. Instructions note when to insert them.
- Leave a long tail (about 12 inches) when fastening off sewing pieces — use this tail to attach parts together.
- Numbers in brackets **[24]** at the end of a round indicate the total stitch count for that round.

---

## Pieces Overview

1. Body (worked from bottom up)
2. Head (worked from top down)
3. Arms × 2
4. Legs × 2
5. Chef Hat (brim + top)
6. Neck Kerchief / Scarf

---

## Part 1 — Body

*Work in white yarn. Stuff firmly as you go.*

**Rnd 1:** MC, 6 sc into ring. Pull tight. **[6]**

**Rnd 2:** Inc in each st around. **[12]**

**Rnd 3:** \*sc 1, inc\* rep around. **[18]**

**Rnd 4:** \*sc 2, inc\* rep around. **[24]**

**Rnd 5:** \*sc 3, inc\* rep around. **[30]**

**Rnd 6:** \*sc 4, inc\* rep around. **[36]**

**Rnd 7:** \*sc 5, inc\* rep around. **[42]**

**Rnds 8–10:** sc in each st around. (3 rounds) **[42]**

**Rnd 11:** \*sc 5, dec\* rep around. **[36]**

**Rnd 12:** sc in each st around. **[36]**

**Rnd 13:** \*sc 4, dec\* rep around. **[30]**

**Rnd 14:** sc in each st around. **[30]**

*(Begin stuffing firmly here. Continue stuffing as you work.)*

**Rnd 15:** \*sc 3, dec\* rep around. **[24]**

**Rnd 16:** sc in each st around. **[24]**

**Rnd 17:** \*sc 2, dec\* rep around. **[18]**

**Rnds 18–20:** sc in each st around. (3 rounds) **[18]**

> **Belly button:** After Rnd 20, use a length of white yarn and a yarn needle to make a small indented stitch at the center front of the body — a simple pinch-and-pull stitch that dimples the fabric inward. This creates the Doughboy's signature belly button. Tie off at the back.

**Rnd 21:** \*sc 1, dec\* rep around. **[12]**

*(Stuff firmly, nearly to the top opening.)*

**Rnd 22:** dec around. **[6]**

Fasten off, leave a 12-inch tail. Use yarn needle to close the opening by weaving through the front loops of the remaining 6 stitches. Pull tight and knot. Weave in end.

## Part 2 — Head

*Work in white yarn. Worked from top down.*

**Rnd 1:** MC, 6 sc into ring. **[6]**

**Rnd 2:** Inc in each st around. **[12]**

**Rnd 3:** \*sc 1, inc\* rep around. **[18]**

**Rnd 4:** \*sc 2, inc\* rep around. **[24]**

**Rnd 5:** \*sc 3, inc\* rep around. **[30]**

**Rnd 6:** \*sc 4, inc\* rep around. **[36]**

**Rnds 7–9:** sc in each st around. (3 rounds) **[36]**

> **Insert safety eyes now:** Position eyes between Rnds 7 and 8, approximately 8–9 stitches apart. Secure backings firmly from inside.

**Rnd 10:** \*sc 4, dec\* rep around. **[30]**

**Rnd 11:** \*sc 3, dec\* rep around. **[24]**

**Rnd 12:** \*sc 2, dec\* rep around. **[18]**

*(Stuff head firmly now.)*

**Rnd 13:** \*sc 1, dec\* rep around. **[12]**

**Rnd 14:** dec around. **[6]**

Fasten off, leave 12-inch tail. Close opening same as body. Weave in end.

### Facial Details

**Mouth:** Using a length of gray or dark yarn and a yarn needle, embroider a small upward-curved smile between and below the eyes. Start at a point one stitch below and two stitches left of center, curve up to center, and back down. Use 2–3 small stitches to form the curve.

**Cheeks (optional):** Use a small amount of light pink yarn or fabric paint/blush to add a rosy tint to each cheek.

**Eyebrows (optional):** Embroider two short curved stitches in light gray or dark yarn just above and slightly outside each eye.

## Part 3 — Arms (Make 2)

*Work in white yarn.*

**Rnd 1:** MC, 6 sc into ring. **[6]**

**Rnd 2:** Inc in each st around. **[12]**

**Rnds 3–4:** sc in each st around. (2 rounds) **[12]**

**Rnd 5:** \*sc 2, dec\* rep around. **[9]**

**Rnds 6–10:** sc in each st around. (5 rounds) **[9]**

*(Lightly stuff the hand/rounded end only. Leave upper arm mostly unstuffed so it lies flat for sewing.)*

**Rnd 11:** \*sc 1, dec\* rep around. **[6]**

Fasten off, leave 12-inch tail for attachment.

> **Hand definition (optional):** Use a yarn needle and a short length of matching yarn to make 3 small "finger" indentations at the rounded end of the arm — simply stitch through from front to back at the base of the hand area and pull gently to define segments.

---

## Part 4 — Legs (Make 2)

*Work in white yarn.*

**Rnd 1:** MC, 6 sc into ring. **[6]**

**Rnd 2:** Inc in each st around. **[12]**

**Rnd 3:** sc in BLO around. **[12]** *(this creates the foot ridge)*

**Rnd 4:** sc in each st around. **[12]**

**Rnd 5:** \*sc 2, dec\* rep around. **[9]**

**Rnds 6–12:** sc in each st around. (7 rounds) **[9]**

*(Stuff firmly, especially the foot.)*

Fasten off, leave 12-inch tail for attachment.

## Part 5 — Chef Hat

### Hat Brim

*Work in white yarn.*

**Rnd 1:** MC, 6 sc. **[6]**

**Rnd 2:** Inc in each st around. **[12]**

**Rnd 3:** \*sc 1, inc\* rep around. **[18]**

**Rnd 4:** \*sc 2, inc\* rep around. **[24]**

**Rnd 5:** sc in BLO around. **[24]** *(creates a ridge for the hat band)*

**Rnds 6–7:** sc in each st around. (2 rounds) **[24]**

### Hat Top

Continue from brim without fasten off:

**Rnd 8:** \*sc 3, dec\* rep around. **[20]**

**Rnd 9:** sc in each st around. **[20]**

**Rnd 10:** sc in each st around. **[20]**

**Rnd 11:** \*sc 3, dec\* rep around. **[16]**

**Rnds 12–14:** sc in each st around. (3 rounds) **[16]**

*(Lightly stuff the top of the hat so it holds its rounded shape.)*

**Rnd 15:** \*sc 2, dec\* rep around. **[12]**

**Rnd 16:** \*sc 1, dec\* rep around. **[8]**

**Rnd 17:** dec around. **[4]**

Fasten off and close opening. Weave in end.

### Hat Band

*Work in gray or silver yarn.*

Ch 26, sl st to first ch to form a ring.

**Rnd 1:** sc in each ch around. **[26]**

**Rnd 2:** sl st in each st around. Fasten off.

Slip band over hat and position at the ridge line between Rnds 4–5. Tack in place with a few stitches.

---

## Part 6 — Neck Kerchief / Scarf

*Work in light blue yarn.*

Ch 40.

**Row 1:** sc in 2nd ch from hook and in each ch across. Turn. **[39]**

**Row 2:** ch 1, sc across. Turn. **[39]**

**Row 3:** ch 1, sc across. Fasten off. **[39]**

Weave in ends. Fold loosely in half lengthwise to form a narrow rectangle. Wrap around the neck area between head and body and tie a small loose knot at front, or tack in place with a few invisible stitches.

---

## Assembly

Follow this order for best results:

**Step 1 — Attach legs to body**
Hold the body with the closed bottom facing down (this is the bottom). Position legs side by side on either side of center bottom. Flatten the open end of each leg slightly. Using the tail end and a yarn needle, whip stitch or mattress stitch each leg firmly to the bottom of the body. Knot and weave in ends securely.

**Step 2 — Attach head to body**
Hold head against the top opening of the body. The head is round with no neck — it sits directly on the body. Use the tail end from the head to sew around the circumference, joining the bottom opening of the head to the top of the body. Add a little extra stuffing to the neck area before closing completely. Knot and weave in.

**Step 3 — Attach arms to body**
Position one arm on each side of the body at approximately Rnd 18–19 (the shoulder area). The arms should angle slightly downward and outward. Sew each arm securely using the tail end. Stitch through body and arm multiple times for stability. Knot and weave in ends.

**Step 4 — Attach hat to head**
Center the hat on the top of the head. Using a length of white yarn and yarn needle, tack the hat brim down around the edge. A few stitches at several points around the brim will hold it securely without it looking stiff.

**Step 5 — Attach kerchief**
Wrap the kerchief around the neck between head and body. Tie or tack as described above.

**Step 6 — Final check**
Inspect all joins and pull any loose ends through to the interior of the piece. Fluff out the shape, adjust stuffing if any area looks thin, and your Doughboy is complete.

## Tips & Customization

- **Giggle button:** To make the Doughboy "giggle" when his belly is poked, you can insert a small squeaker toy (found at craft stores) inside the body before closing. Make sure it is fully surrounded by fiberfill so it doesn't shift.

- **Standing:** Stuff the legs extra firmly and flatten the foot base so the figure can stand on its own. A small square of cardboard or plastic canvas cut to fit inside the foot can help stability.

- **Stiffer hat:** Spray the finished hat lightly with liquid starch or fabric stiffener and let dry in shape before attaching.

- **Size adjustment:** Using bulky (size 5 or 6) yarn with a larger hook produces a bigger, chunkier Doughboy. Using sport or DK weight with a smaller hook makes a miniature version.

- **Washing:** Because of the safety eyes and fiberfill, hand wash only in cool water and lay flat to dry.
