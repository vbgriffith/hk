# ODRC Commissary Cookbook
### Recipes Made from Common Ohio Prison Commissary Items

> **Note:** All recipes use items commonly available at ODRC facility commissaries — no outside ingredients required. Cooking methods are limited to hot water, microwaves, and heat-safe containers. All measurements are approximate.

---

## Common Commissary Pantry

The following items commonly appear on ODRC commissary lists and are the building blocks for all recipes in this book:

**Starches & Grains**
- Ramen noodles (chicken, beef, chili, shrimp flavors)
- Instant rice packets
- Crackers (saltines, peanut butter crackers)
- Corn chips / Fritos
- Tortilla chips / Doritos

**Proteins**
- Mackerel / Jack Mack (canned)
- Tuna packets or cans
- Summer sausage / pepperoni sticks
- Slim Jims
- Peanut butter

**Dairy & Spreads**
- Squeeze cheese / jalapeño cheese spread
- Mayonnaise packets
- Peanut butter crackers

**Condiments & Seasonings**
- Hot sauce
- Mustard packets
- Salsa packets
- Ramen seasoning packets
- Salt & pepper packets

**Sweets & Snacks**
- Little Debbie cakes / honey buns
- Candy (Jolly Ranchers, etc.)
- Popcorn

**Beverages**
- Instant coffee
- Kool-Aid / drink mix packets
- Mountain Dew / soda (where available)

## The Recipes

---

### 1. Chi Chi (The Classic)
*The most iconic commissary recipe in the American prison system. Customizable and communal.*

**Ingredients:**
- 2 packs ramen noodles
- 1 summer sausage or pepperoni stick, sliced
- 1 packet squeeze cheese or jalapeño cheese
- Hot sauce to taste
- 1–2 ramen seasoning packets

**Instructions:**
1. Crush the ramen noodles while still in their packages.
2. Open packages and pour crushed noodles into a large heat-safe bag or container.
3. Add just enough boiling or very hot water to cover the noodles. Seal and let sit 5–7 minutes.
4. Drain off excess water carefully.
5. Mix in one ramen seasoning packet, cheese spread, and sliced sausage.
6. Add hot sauce to taste.
7. Mix well and eat directly from the bag or pour into a bowl.

**Tips:** Add crushed chips or crackers for extra texture. Tuna or mackerel can replace or supplement the sausage.

### 2. Jailhouse Pad Thai
*A surprisingly tasty noodle dish with just three ingredients.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 pack ramen noodles (any flavor)
- 2 tablespoons peanut butter
- Hot sauce to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Cook ramen noodles in hot water as directed (about 3–5 minutes).
2. Drain most of the water, leaving a small amount.
3. Stir in peanut butter until it melts into a sauce.
4. Add hot sauce and mix well.
5. Optionally add the ramen seasoning packet (half, to taste).

**Tips:** Crushed peanut butter crackers can be crumbled on top for a garnish. Tuna added in makes this more filling.

---

### 3. Prison Pizza
*A creative ramen-and-cracker crust pizza.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1–2 packs ramen noodles
- 1 sleeve saltine crackers, crushed fine
- Salsa packet (for sauce)
- Squeeze cheese
- Summer sausage or pepperoni, thinly sliced
- Hot water

**Instructions:**
1. Crush ramen noodles and crackers together into a fine mix in a bag.
2. Add hot water slowly and knead the bag until a thick dough forms. Drain any excess water.
3. Press the dough flat in the bag or onto a flat surface into a circle — this is your crust.
4. Spread salsa over the crust as the sauce.
5. Top with squeeze cheese and sliced sausage/pepperoni.
6. Let sit a few minutes for flavors to meld. Eat with a spoon or break apart with hands.

**Tips:** The thinner the crust, the crispier it gets as it cools. Jalapeño cheese gives it extra kick.

### 4. Chip Tamales
*A no-cook corn snack that requires nothing but chips and hot water.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 bag Fritos or corn chips
- 1 bag Flamin' Hot Cheetos or spicy chips
- Hot sauce
- Hot water

**Instructions:**
1. Combine both bags of chips into one large bag.
2. Crush the chips thoroughly by squeezing the bag.
3. Add hot water slowly — just enough to make a thick, moist mush. Avoid making it watery.
4. Knead the bag until everything is evenly combined.
5. Roll the mixture inside the chip bag into a tight tamale shape.
6. Let sit 5 minutes, then peel back the bag and drizzle with hot sauce.

**Tips:** This works with any combination of chips. Doritos add a cheesy flavor without any extra cheese needed.

### 5. Tuna Ramen Noodle Casserole
*A protein-packed one-bag meal.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 pack ramen noodles (chicken flavor)
- 1 packet or can of tuna
- 2 mayo packets
- 1 mustard packet
- Hot sauce
- Crushed crackers (optional)

**Instructions:**
1. Cook ramen noodles in hot water. Drain most liquid.
2. In a separate cup or bag, mix tuna, mayo, mustard, and a dash of hot sauce into a spread.
3. Add tuna mixture to the noodles and mix well.
4. Use half the ramen seasoning packet to season.
5. Top with crushed crackers for crunch if desired.

**Tips:** Swap tuna for mackerel (Jack Mack) for a stronger flavor. This also works cold if hot water isn't available.

### 6. Mackerel & Noodle Bowl
*Inspired by Ja Rule's go-to prison meal.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 pack ramen noodles (any flavor)
- 1 can/packet mackerel (Jack Mack)
- Hot sauce or salsa
- Squeeze cheese (optional)
- Crushed Doritos (optional)

**Instructions:**
1. Cook ramen noodles with hot water. Drain.
2. Open mackerel and drain the liquid. Flake the fish apart.
3. Combine noodles and mackerel in a bag or bowl.
4. Add hot sauce or salsa and mix well.
5. Top with squeeze cheese and crushed Doritos for texture.

**Tips:** The Doritos add salt and spice. Use the ramen seasoning packet sparingly — mackerel is already salty.

### 7. The Spread
*A communal feast dish — designed to feed multiple people using everyone's contributions.*

**Ingredients:**
- 2–3 packs ramen noodles
- 1 can tuna or mackerel
- 1 bag chips (any kind)
- Hot sauce
- Salsa packets
- Squeeze cheese
- Pepperoni or summer sausage slices
- Any extras on hand (crackers, popcorn, etc.)

**Instructions:**
1. Cook ramen noodles and drain.
2. Lay everything out on a spread of newspaper or a clean flat bag.
3. Start with noodles as the base and top with fish, meat, cheese, salsa, and chips.
4. Drizzle hot sauce generously over everything.
5. Mix together or eat in layers — each person takes a portion.

**Tips:** This is traditionally a communal recipe shared between cellmates or pod mates. The more people contribute, the better it gets.

### 8. Peanut Butter Honey Bun Cake
*A simple sweet treat using commissary dessert staples.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 honey bun or Little Debbie cake
- 2 tablespoons peanut butter
- Kool-Aid powder (optional, for color and flavor)

**Instructions:**
1. Unwrap the honey bun and place in a bowl or on a flat surface.
2. Spread peanut butter generously over the top.
3. If desired, mix a small amount of Kool-Aid powder with a few drops of water to make a glaze and drizzle over the top.
4. Let sit 2 minutes and enjoy.

**Tips:** Strawberry or grape Kool-Aid pairs surprisingly well with peanut butter. This also works with oatmeal cakes or any soft snack cake.

### 9. Ramen Seasoning Broth
*When you want something warm but simple.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1–2 ramen seasoning packets
- Hot water (8–10 oz)
- Salt, hot sauce (optional)

**Instructions:**
1. Bring water to as hot as possible.
2. Pour into a cup and stir in one seasoning packet.
3. Add hot sauce or a pinch of salt to taste.
4. Sip as a broth or soup.

**Tips:** This is a go-to for warming up in cold months or when commissary funds are low. Chicken flavor makes the most convincing broth.

---

### 10. Cracker Nachos
*A quick snack that comes together in under two minutes.*

**Ingredients:**
- 1 sleeve saltine crackers
- Squeeze cheese or jalapeño cheese spread
- Hot sauce
- Sliced summer sausage or pepperoni (optional)

**Instructions:**
1. Lay crackers out flat in a container or on a clean bag.
2. Squeeze cheese generously over crackers.
3. Top with sliced sausage if available.
4. Dash hot sauce over everything.
5. Eat immediately.

**Tips:** Doritos or corn chips can substitute for or supplement the crackers. This is one of the fastest and easiest commissary snacks.

## General Tips for Commissary Cooking

- **Hot water is your oven.** Almost every recipe here relies on boiling or very hot water. If a microwave is available, it opens up more options.
- **Seasoning packets are currency.** Ramen flavor packets double as all-purpose seasoning for nearly any dish.
- **Bags are your bowls.** Large plastic bags work well for mixing, cooking, and serving — and are easier to clean up.
- **Less water is usually better.** When in doubt, use less water than you think you need. You can always add more; you can't take it out.
- **Protein stretches everything.** A small amount of tuna, mackerel, or sausage makes a ramen dish far more filling and satisfying.
- **Budget wisely.** Combine with others when possible — communal cooking makes commissary funds go further and meals taste better.
